What's Jenny up to lately?

March–June 2020

  • Sooo... the world is a little different since I updated last... *screams internally & externally*
  • Wrestling with how to be Out In The World when it's not always fair for folks on the other side (read: going to restaurants or the supermarket puts a burden on the people working more than it does me.)
  • We're ordering takeout and buying things from small businesses we <3 through mail and contactless pickup. It's diversified our buying habits and made us (I think) more mindful about where our money is going and why. Shouts to a couple of our local businesses, who have kept us fed, happy and properly masked over the past few months: Allium Market, gather here, Stoked & the Publick House.
  • Currently listening to Stamped from the Beginning – The Definitive History of Racist Ideas in America by Ibram X. Kendi on Spotify. Might need to just buy a hard copy because it's great but a suuuper information-dense text that I want to highlight/reference. (P.S., did you know that Ibram X. Kendi is now running a center out of Boston University?)
  • Brian and I are both deep into Animal Crossing. Message me for a friend code. I'm reading up on the best ways to get hybrid flowers... turns out the game mechanics around flower breeding are based in real plant genetics, which is delightful.
  • Through all of this, we celebrated one year of marriage. We did not go out, and we have yet to have a "real" honeymoon (hello, 2021? It's me, Jenny. I would like a tropical drink on a beach somewhere far away, please.) You *could* say that us holing up in our apartment together... basically all the time... is its own kind of honeymoon, but you'd be wrong! JK, we are still getting along famously. We've also taken up making cocktails at home, so there's that. Cheers. <3
  • I miss live music, a LOT. But some of our friends are doing wonderful things in this gap. Namely: We're About 9 and Jason Anderson. They keep us sane, and we miss them a lot.
